## How to Use This File

Give this to your AI when designing emails. Each entry includes structured design notes
so your AI understands *why* each design works, not just *what* it looks like.

- **Browse by category** to find patterns for your use case
- **Reference specific entries** when asking your AI to design an email
- **Combine patterns** from multiple entries for unique designs
- **Use the "steal this" notes** as direct instructions for your AI

**Example prompt:** "Design a welcome email for my SaaS product. Use the Figma pattern
(#1) for product-led onboarding, but with the MoMA approach (#4) to white space."

## Welcome & Onboarding

### 1. Figma - Product-Led Welcome

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Subject line:** "Welcome to Figma!"
- **Why it converts:** Purple gradients and product-mirroring design. The email feels like opening the app for the first time. Clear getting-started CTAs without clutter. Sets the bar for SaaS welcome emails.
- **Steal this:** Mirror your product's visual language in your welcome email. Make onboarding steps feel like using the product, not reading about it.
- **Tags:** saas, welcome, onboarding, design-tool, product-led
- **Source:** [Figma](https://reallygoodemails.com/emails/welcome-to-figma)

### 2. Claude - Award-Winning Welcome Email

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Why it converts:** Won the Welcome Email category at Really Good Email Awards 2025. Judges praised the branding and clear sense of purpose. Sophisticated AI brand translated into warm, approachable email.
- **Steal this:** Lead with purpose, not features. Even technical products should feel human in the inbox.
- **Outcome:** RGE 2025 Award Winner - Welcome Email category
- **Tags:** award-winner, ai, saas, branding
- **Source:** [Claude (Anthropic)](https://reallygoodemails.com/2025-email-awards)

### 4. MoMA - Gallery-Quality Welcome

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Subject line:** "Welcome from MoMA!"
- **Why it converts:** Treats the email itself as a piece of art. Gallery-quality imagery, restrained typography, generous white space. Non-commercial approach that makes you feel like a patron, not a customer.
- **Steal this:** Use white space with the confidence of an art gallery. Let imagery breathe. Restraint is a design choice.
- **Tags:** culture, elegant, typography, white-space, art
- **Source:** [MoMA](https://reallygoodemails.com/emails/welcome-from-moma)

### 5. Lyka - Pet Name Personalization Welcome

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Subject line:** "Smiles, welcome to the Lyka Pack!"
- **Why it converts:** Uses the pet's name in the subject line, not the owner's. Community language ('the Pack') builds belonging from first touch. Warm illustrations and personality-driven copy.
- **Steal this:** Personalize with unexpected data points. The pet's name is more emotional than the owner's name. Build community language early.
- **Tags:** dtc, pets, personalization, community, personality
- **Source:** [Lyka](https://reallygoodemails.com/emails/welcome-to-the-lyka-pack)

### 6. Headspace - Mindful Onboarding

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Why it converts:** Distinctive cartoon characters. No sharp edges anywhere in the design for a softer, friendlier feel. Every color and shape is intentional. The email IS the brand experience. Tailored onboarding paths for active vs inactive users.
- **Steal this:** Your email design should feel like using your product. If your product is calm, your email should be calm. Match the emotional register.
- **Outcome:** Welcome emails generate 4x more opens, 5x more clicks vs regular campaigns; 50% average open rate
- **Tags:** wellness, illustration, onboarding, habit-building, friendly
- **Source:** [Headspace](https://emailmastery.org/teardown/the-headspace-email-marketing-teardown/)

### 7. Hyggekrog - Small Brand, 90% Open Rate

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Why it converts:** A small personal brand achieving a 90% open rate and 32.94% click rate. Purple background with clean layout. Founder personally writes every email. Proof that authenticity beats production value.
- **Steal this:** Founder-written emails outperform corporate ones. Be a person. Set expectations clearly. Include a discount to drive first purchase.
- **Outcome:** 90% open rate, 32.94% click rate
- **Tags:** small-business, high-open-rate, personal-brand, founder-written
- **Source:** [Hyggekrog](https://www.mailerlite.com/blog/email-marketing-examples)

### 8. Who Gives A Crap - Values-Driven Welcome

- **Use case:** Welcome email
- **Why it converts:** Creative footer design with playful brand icons and a sincere land acknowledgement. Social links spaced for breathing room. The footer alone builds brand respect. Even the parts nobody reads are beautiful.
- **Steal this:** Design every element, including the footer. Values-driven messaging can differentiate even in the most unglamorous categories.
- **Tags:** values-driven, footer-design, sustainability, dtc
- **Source:** [Who Gives A Crap](https://www.klaviyo.com/blog/email-design-examples)

## Product Launches

### 9. Apple - iPad Air M4 Launch

- **Use case:** Product Launch email
- **Subject line:** "Upgrade to the new iPad Air. Now supercharged by M4."
- **Why it converts:** Masterclass in letting the product speak for itself. Stunning photography on clean background. Every pixel serves the product. Massive white space. The dual CTA (buy/learn more) gives choice without clutter.
- **Steal this:** If your product looks good, get out of its way. One hero image, minimal copy, maximum impact.
- **Tags:** tech, premium, minimal, photography, dual-cta
- **Source:** [Apple](https://reallygoodemails.com/emails/new-ipad-air-m4)

### 10. Linear - The Gold Standard Changelog

- **Use case:** Changelog email
- **Why it converts:** The benchmark for SaaS product updates. Bold, concise headlines. GIFs showing features in action. Benefit-driven language, not jargon. Weekly summaries that people actually want to read.
- **Steal this:** Use GIFs to show features, not describe them. Write headlines that describe the benefit, not the feature. Make changelogs feel like content, not documentation.
- **Tags:** saas, changelog, developer-focused, gifs, scannable
- **Source:** [Linear](https://blog.getsimpledirect.com/linears-changelog-strategy-a-deep-dive-and-what-you-can-learn/)

- **Tags:** ai, product-launch, bold-typography, urgency, tech
- **Source:** [Gamma](https://reallygoodemails.com/emails/gamma-imagine-launches-today)
